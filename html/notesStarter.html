    The first thing I'll do is include some of the miscellaneous facts which are somewhat of interest to me. I would say a decent amount of this is learning some of the terminology, specifically seeing the cards for 1 pass so I can get the right phrasings in place. I do believe this will help me and I'm a little surprised I didn't do this while I was studying my second time.
  • Elements with unpaired electrons are paramagnetic
  • Chromium and Copper (Cr and Cu) pull an extra e- from 4s to fill their 3d
  • Transformation is the uptake of DNA fragments from the environment into the bacterium
  • The alpha carbon of every AA is L (S)
  • Radioactive decay follows first-order kinetics
  • Decay is expressed as N_t = N_0 exp(-kt)
  • Phospholipids are triglycerols with a phosphate group in place of ONE of the fatty acids
  • The liver converts almost all digested carbohydrates to glucose
  • The liver is one of the few places glycogen can be converted to glucose
  • Cells absorb glucose through facilitated diffusion
  • Myoglobin is what carries the oxygen in muscles and organs
  • Proteoglycans consist of more than 50% carbohydrates and are the major component of the extracellular matrix
  • Cytochromes are proteins that require a prosthetic heme group
